## Section 5 — Development Process

### H2:
From Idea to Working AI Agent

### Intro line:
A process built for speed, reliability, and trust — with you involved at every stage.

---

**Step 1 — Discovery & Use-Case Scoping** *(Week 1–2)*
We start by understanding your workflows, tools, and goals. Together we identify where an agent will deliver the most value, define success metrics, and choose the right approach — so we build something that pays off, not just something that demos well.

**Step 2 — Agent Design & Architecture** *(Week 2–3)*
We design how your agent reasons, what actions it can take, what data it can access, and where humans stay in the loop. We select the right models, define guardrails, and map every integration before development begins.

**Step 3 — Agent Development** *(Week 3–8)*
We build in agile sprints, with a working agent you can test at the end of each cycle. You see real behaviour early, and we refine prompts, logic, and integrations based on how the agent performs on your actual tasks.

**Step 4 — Testing, Guardrails & Evaluation**
AI agents need a different kind of QA. We rigorously test for accuracy, reliability, edge cases, and safety — running evaluations across real scenarios to ensure your agent behaves predictably and fails gracefully when it should.

**Step 5 — Deployment & Integration**
We deploy your agent into your live environment, connect it securely to your tools, and set up monitoring so you can see exactly what it's doing. We stay close during the critical first weeks of real-world use.

**Step 6 — Monitor, Improve & Scale**
Agents get better with feedback. We track performance, refine behaviour, expand capabilities, and add new use cases as you see what's working — turning a single agent into a growing automation advantage.

## Bonus Section — FAQ

### H2:
Frequently Asked Questions About AI Agent Development

*(Add FAQPage schema markup to this section)*

---

**What is an AI agent?**
An AI agent is a software system powered by AI that can understand a goal, reason through the steps to achieve it, and take actions across your tools and data — not just respond to questions. Unlike a basic chatbot, an agent can do real work: updating records, sending messages, processing documents, and completing multi-step tasks with minimal human input.

---

**What's the difference between an AI agent and a chatbot?**
A chatbot mainly holds a conversation and answers questions. An AI agent takes action. Agents can connect to your systems, make decisions based on context, use tools, and complete tasks autonomously — for example, qualifying a lead, updating your CRM, and scheduling a follow-up, all on its own.

---

**How much does AI agent development cost?**
Cost depends on the complexity of the workflow, the number of integrations, and the level of autonomy required. A focused single-workflow agent is significantly less than a coordinated multi-agent system. During your strategy call, we provide a detailed estimate tailored to your specific use case and goals.

---

**How long does it take to build an AI agent?**
A focused agent automating a single workflow can often be live in a few weeks. More complex or multi-agent systems with several integrations typically take a few months. Timelines depend on scope, the systems we're connecting to, and how quickly decisions are made on your side.

---

**Can AI agents integrate with our existing tools?**
Yes — that's the core of what we do. We connect agents to CRMs, ERPs, helpdesks, databases, payment systems, and productivity tools through APIs and native integrations, so your agent can read and act across the systems you already use.

---

**How do you make sure AI agents are reliable and safe?**
We build in guardrails, human-in-the-loop checkpoints, and rigorous evaluation from the start. Agents are tested across real scenarios and edge cases, given clear limits on what they can access and do, and monitored in production — so they behave predictably and escalate to humans when appropriate.

---

**Do you sign NDAs before discussing our project?**
Yes, always. We're happy to sign a mutual NDA before any strategy call or proposal discussion. Your idea and business information are treated with complete confidentiality.
